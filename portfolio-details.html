Process

My design problem is pedestrian pushbuttons at traffic intersections, that exclude people with red-green colour blindness, people with visual impairments, and people with physical disabilities.
Remedies to The Exclusionary Features
I designed a sensor pad that can be placed on the curb at all intersections in complement to the existing traffic signal buttons and tactile paving.
The sensor pad remedies the design problem in the way that everyone can access the pushbuttons with the least physical and cognitive efforts.
By staying upon a sensor pad for more than 30 seconds, the sensor will activate the appropriate pushbuttons depending on their facing direction.
Once the pushbuttons have been successfully activated, the sensor pad will slightly vibrate, and the pushbuttons will buzz off.

  • People with visual impairments will naturally be directed and stop upon the sensor pad when they follow the existing tactile paving.
  • The sensor pad provides another form of signal for them to discern if the pushbutton is functioning and activated for red-green colour blinded people.
  • Without forced to access the pushbuttons in an uncomfortable way, the psychological barriers are mitigated. The structural barriers that people with physical disabilities face are addressed by completely removing the process of going through manually push the buttons.
